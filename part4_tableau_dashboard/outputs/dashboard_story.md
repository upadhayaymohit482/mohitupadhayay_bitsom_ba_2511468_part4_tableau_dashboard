# Dashboard Story — Executive Sales Performance Report
## Retail Leadership Team | Fiscal Period: January 2024 – December 2025

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Over the two-year period from January 2024 to December 2025, the business generated **₹217 million in total sales** and **₹33.3 million in total profit**, representing a blended profit margin of approximately **15.3%**. The business is healthy at a macro level, driven predominantly by the Technology category and the South and North regions. However, a closer look reveals two systemic risks that require leadership attention: **aggressive discounting in Furniture** that is generating net losses on individual orders, and a **7.7% return rate in Furniture** that is eroding profitability and adding hidden logistics costs.

The dashboard is designed to help leadership monitor performance across five dimensions simultaneously: sales trends, regional health, category profitability, customer segment behavior, and operational efficiency (shipping and returns). Together, these views tell a coherent story of where the business is strong, where margin is leaking, and where the highest-potential growth opportunities lie.

---

## 2. What is Performing Well

**Technology is the backbone of profitability.** Accounting for 71% of total revenue (₹153.9M) and an 18.2% profit margin — the highest of any category — Technology is the primary value driver. Within Technology, Copiers, Accessories, and Phones are the standout sub-categories. The Copiers sub-category in particular generates extraordinary profit on large individual orders, suggesting an enterprise sales strength worth nurturing.

**The South region is the commercial leader.** With ₹64.7M in sales and ₹9.99M in profit, the South outperforms every other region. Cities like Hyderabad, Bengaluru, and Chennai are driving strong demand across Technology and Office Supplies. The region's sales team and channel mix appear to be performing optimally.

**Low-discount orders are highly profitable.** Orders with a discount of 0–10% generate an 18.9% profit margin on ₹123.4M in combined revenue. This demonstrates that when the business sells at or near list price, it is highly profitable. The challenge is discipline in the field.

**Home Office and Consumer segments show steady demand.** All three customer segments perform within a narrow 15.2–15.5% margin band, indicating a well-diversified customer base with no single segment over-dependent on the business.

---

## 3. What is Underperforming

**Furniture is a structural margin problem.** Despite ₹51.6M in sales, Furniture contributes only ₹3.6M in profit — a 6.9% margin, less than half of Technology's. Within Furniture, Tables (5.7% margin) and Bookcases (5.7% margin) are near-breakeven sub-categories. At discount levels above 30%, multiple individual Furniture orders record negative profit, meaning the business is losing money on those transactions.

**The West region is an underperformer.** While the West generates a reasonable ₹48.9M in sales, its profit (₹7.4M) is the lowest of all four regions. This may reflect a less favorable product mix, a higher concentration of discounted Furniture orders, or underdeveloped Technology penetration in states like Goa and Madhya Pradesh.

**Mid-year months are reliably weak.** April–May and August are the three weakest months across both years, with sales dipping to ₹6.3M–₹7.5M. Without proactive intervention, these slow periods repeat predictably and represent recovered revenue that the business is leaving on the table.

---

## 4. What Risks are Visible

**Discount indiscipline at 30–35% is creating loss-making orders.** The data shows that the 30–35% discount tier produced a cumulative net loss of ₹102,000 across ₹2.07M in orders. While small in absolute terms, this discount tier represents a policy failure: the business is actively accepting orders where it loses money. Without a hard cap, this risk can compound as sales volumes grow.

**Furniture return rate of 7.7% is a dual threat.** Returns represent both a revenue reversal and an incremental logistics cost. At 7.67% — more than double Technology's 3.03% — Furniture returns suggest systemic quality or expectation issues. If unaddressed, this compounds Furniture's already thin margin into potential negative territory at scale.

**Standard Class delivery averaging 4.71 days may be a competitive vulnerability.** In a market where same-day and next-day delivery is increasingly standard, a nearly 5-day delivery average on the most-used shipping mode could be influencing customer satisfaction and driving the above-average return rate.

---

## 5. What Opportunities are Visible

**Technology up-sell within the Home Office segment.** Home Office is the most profitable segment and also the largest by sales. If the Technology penetration within Home Office can be increased through targeted outreach (email campaigns via the already-proven Email channel), the incremental margin impact would be significant.

**Replicate South region's success in the East.** The East region (₹48.9M, ₹7.6M profit) underperforms the South by a wide margin despite having comparable geography and population. The South's sales playbook — likely a stronger Technology focus and better enterprise relationships — could be adapted and deployed in East region cities like Kolkata, Patna, and Bhubaneswar.

**Same Day and First Class shipping as premium revenue streams.** Same Day shipping (₹14.6M sales, ₹2.19M profit) and First Class (₹31.9M sales, ₹4.64M profit) both maintain strong margins while delivering superior customer experience. Expanding the adoption of these tiers — particularly for Technology orders — can increase both revenue and margin simultaneously.

**Seasonal campaign opportunity in Q2 and August.** The mid-year dips (April–May, August) are predictable enough to plan proactive promotional campaigns. Targeted discounts in the 10–20% range (which maintain positive margins) during these months could smooth revenue and reduce the volatility in the trend line.

---

## 6. Recommended Business Actions

1. **Implement a discount governance policy immediately.** Cap field discounts at 25% for all categories without manager approval. Remove the 30–35% discount tier from the standard sales toolkit. This single action has the potential to eliminate loss-making orders entirely.

2. **Prioritize Technology sales in the West region.** Commission a targeted campaign to increase Technology penetration in West region cities (Ahmedabad, Mumbai, Indore, Udaipur). Benchmark against the South region's product mix as the success template.

3. **Investigate and remediate Furniture return drivers.** Conduct a root-cause analysis of Furniture returns (specifically Chairs, Tables, Bookcases). Improve product photography, description accuracy, and delivery packaging. Set a quarterly target to reduce Furniture return rate to below 5%.

4. **Design Q2 revenue recovery campaigns.** Plan promotional activities for April and May each year, anchored by bundles and modest discounts (10–15%) in Technology and Office Supplies. Monitor and measure impact on monthly sales.

5. **Develop a Home Office Technology bundle offer.** Create a curated Technology bundle (e.g., Phone + Accessories + Copier) for the Home Office segment distributed via the Email channel, which already shows strong performance in this segment.

---

## 7. Limitations of the Dashboard

- **No customer-level data:** The dashboard aggregates by segment rather than by individual customer, so high-value customer concentration risk (e.g., one customer driving 30% of Corporate revenue) is not visible.
- **No cost breakdown:** The "Cost" field is derived as Sales minus Profit and does not distinguish between COGS, shipping costs, and returns processing costs. A more granular cost model would improve the accuracy of profitability analysis.
- **Return data is binary:** The return_flag field indicates whether an order was returned but does not capture the reason code (quality, wrong item, damage in transit). Root-cause analysis of returns requires additional data collection.
- **Campaign channel has missing values:** Approximately 3–5% of orders have no campaign_channel recorded. This limits the accuracy of channel attribution analysis.
- **Geographic granularity is at state level:** City-level and pin-code-level analysis would provide more actionable targeting for the sales and logistics teams.

---

## 8. Suggested Next Analysis

1. **Customer lifetime value (CLV) analysis** — Segment customers by CLV to identify high-value customers at risk and low-value customers consuming disproportionate service resources.
2. **Cohort analysis by acquisition channel** — Compare the long-term profitability of customers acquired through Organic, Paid, Email, Referral, and Social channels to optimize marketing spend.
3. **Product-level profitability analysis** — Move from sub-category to individual SKU profitability to identify specific products that should be discontinued or repriced.
4. **Delivery delay impact on customer rating** — Correlate delivery days with customer_rating to quantify the financial value of faster shipping in terms of repeat purchase probability.
5. **Return cost modelling** — Integrate actual return processing costs to calculate the true cost of the 4.55% return rate and build a business case for quality improvement investments.
